# Planets

The galaxy contains thousands of planets across ~400 star systems. Each planet has a type, habitability rating, and available resources.

## Planet Types

| Type | Habitability | Description | Resources |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| Terran | 100% | Earth-like with breathable atmosphere | Water, Organics, Food |
| Oceanic | 70% | Covered almost entirely by water | Water, Food, Bio-Samples |
| Jungle | 80% | Dense vegetation covering most surface | Organics, Bio-Samples, Food, Seeds |
| Desert | 50% | Hot, arid world with sparse water | Rare Minerals, Ore, Silicon |
| Ice | 30% | Frozen world with subsurface oceans | Water, Chemicals, Gases |
| Volcanic | 20% | Geologically active with extreme heat | Ore, Plasma, Rare Minerals |
| Barren | 10% | Rocky, lifeless with minimal atmosphere | Ore, Refined Metals, Construction Materials |
| Toxic | 10% | Atmosphere filled with harmful gases | Chemicals, Plasma, Narcotics |
| Crystalline | 20% | Surface covered in crystal formations | Rare Minerals, Electronics, AI Cores |
| Gas Giant | 0% | Massive gas planet, no solid surface | Fuel, Chemicals, Plasma |

### Habitability

Habitability determines how easy it is to establish and maintain a colony:
- **100%** (Terran) — Ideal. Fastest colony development.
- **50–80%** — Moderate. Slower development, higher costs.
- **10–30%** — Harsh. Very slow development, limited population.
- **0%** (Gas Giant) — Cannot be colonized.

## Star Classes and Planets

What planets appear in a system depends on the star class. See [Navigation](?article=navigation) for the full star class table.

**Best systems for colonization:**
- **Class G (Yellow)** — Up to 15 planets. Highest chance of Terran, Oceanic, and Jungle worlds.
- **Class F (Yellow-White)** — Up to 10 planets. Good mix including Terran and Oceanic.

**Best systems for mining:**
- **Class O/B (Blue)** — Volcanic and Barren worlds rich in Ore and Plasma.
- **Class K (Orange)** — Desert worlds with Rare Minerals.
- **Neutron Star** — Rare Crystalline worlds with Electronics and AI Cores.

## Extractable Resources

Planets contain extractable resources that colonies can harvest:

| Resource | Base Yield | Rarity |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Water | 15 | 70% |
| Organics | 12 | 50% |
| Iron Ore | 10 | 80% |
| Silicon | 8 | 60% |
| Fuel | 6 | 40% |
| Chemicals | 5 | 30% |
| Rare Minerals | 3 | 20% |
| Bio-Samples | 2 | 15% |

> **Tip**: Rare Minerals and Bio-Samples are the most valuable extractable resources but also the rarest. Crystalline and Jungle planets are the best places to find them.

## Orbital Position

Planets occupy orbital positions 1–15 from their star:

- **Positions 1–3** (inner): More likely to be Volcanic, Desert, or Barren
- **Positions 4–9** (mid): Best chance for Terran, Oceanic, Jungle
- **Positions 10–15** (outer): More likely to be Ice, Gas Giant

Natural gaps exist between orbital positions, so not every position will have a planet.
