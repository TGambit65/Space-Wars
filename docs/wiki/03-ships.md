# Ships

Space Wars 3000 features 13 ship types, each designed for a specific role. Ships differ in hull strength, shield capacity, fuel tank, cargo space, and crew capacity.

## Ship Roles

### Combat Ships
- **Fighter** — Highest weapon slots (3), fast and agile. Best for pure combat.
- **Corvette** — Good balance of weapons and defense. Versatile combatant.
- **Destroyer** — 4 weapon slots, 3 shield slots, 3 armor slots. Serious firepower.
- **Battlecruiser** — 5 weapon slots. The most dangerous combat ship. Requires Capital Class Ships tech.
- **Interceptor** — 3 engine slots for maximum speed. Great for chasing or fleeing.

### Trade Ships
- **Merchant Cruiser** — 3 cargo pod slots with decent defenses. The everyday trader.
- **Freighter** — 5 cargo pod slots for massive hauling capacity. Slow but profitable.
- **Mining Barge** — 6 cargo pod slots and 800 base cargo. Built for resource extraction.

### Exploration Ships
- **Scout** — 2 scanner slots. Your starting ship, good for scouting.
- **Explorer** — 3 scanner slots, 2 engine slots, 200 fuel. The ultimate survey vessel.

### Colony Ships
- **Colony Ship** — 1000 cargo, 20 crew capacity. Consumed when founding a colony. 8-hour development timer.
- **Insta Colony Ship** — 500 cargo, 15 crew. Instant colonization but no weapons.

### Capital Ships
- **Carrier** — 4 armor slots, 4 shield slots, 15 crew. Mobile fortress with 3 scanner slots.

## Choosing a Ship

Consider your play style:

> **Traders** should start with a Merchant Cruiser, then upgrade to a Freighter or Mining Barge for maximum profits.

> **Warriors** should aim for a Corvette early, then a Destroyer or Battlecruiser once they've built up credits.

> **Explorers** should invest in an Explorer with top-tier scanners and engines for efficient galaxy surveying.
