01
New Player Onboarding
P0 CRITICAL

After registration and faction selection, the player lands on a dashboard with no guidance. No tutorial, no tooltips, no first-mission prompt. The sidebar has 23+ nav items — overwhelming for a new player.

Industry data shows that games lose 60-80% of new players within the first 5 minutes if there's no guided experience. Your game has deep systems (trading, combat, colonization, crafting, crew, factions), but a brand-new player sees all 23 menu items at once with zero indication of where to start. The "Explore. Trade. Conquer." tagline on the login screen is the only guidance they ever get. Every successful MMO gates complexity behind progression — World of Warcraft didn't show you raids on day one.

First-login tutorial flow — A short guided sequence: "Check your ship → Navigate to a port → Make your first trade → Scan a planet." Track completion via a tutorial_step field on the User model. Each step highlights the relevant sidebar item and shows a brief tooltip explaining the page.

Medium effort

"Getting Started" dashboard card — For new players (level 1), show a checklist card at the top of the dashboard with 3-4 items linking to their first actions. Disappears once completed or dismissed.

Low effort

Progressive sidebar disclosure — Hide Automation, Crafting, Corporations, Outposts, Events, and Wiki until the player reaches the relevant level or tech prerequisite. Show a locked "More Features" section with unlock hints. This cuts the initial nav from 23 to ~12 items.

Medium effort

Contextual empty states — When a player visits Trading with no port nearby, the current "No trading port in this sector" message is decent, but add: "Nearest port is in Sector X (2 jumps away)" with a link to the map.

Low effort
02
Navigation & Spatial Awareness
P0 CRITICAL

Galaxy map, system view, and sector map are separate full-page views with no persistent spatial context. When a player switches to trading, combat, or colony pages, they lose all sense of where they are in the universe.

Spatial awareness is the backbone of any exploration game. Right now, the sidebar shows the player's credits and faction, but not their location or fuel. A player on the Trading page has no idea what sector they're in without navigating away. Additionally, the backend defines 5 types of space phenomena (ion storms, nebulae, asteroid fields, solar flares, gravity wells) with real gameplay effects like shield reduction and flee prevention — but none of them are visible anywhere in the UI. Players are being penalized by invisible hazards they can't see or plan around.

Persistent location bar — At the top of every non-map page, show: Sector: Alpha Centauri | Port: Trading Hub | Fuel: 72/100. Always visible, always current.

Low effort

Mini-map widget — Collapsible bottom-left corner widget showing the immediate sector neighborhood with adjacent sectors clickable for quick travel.

Medium effort

System View interaction hints — Show visual indicators for interactable entities (ports = dock icon, planets = scan icon, NPCs = hail/attack). Currently players must click each entity to discover what actions are available.

Low effort

Space phenomena on maps — Render ion storms, nebulae, asteroid fields, solar flares, and gravity wells as colored overlays or sector badges with tooltip descriptions. The backend has full definitions for all 5 types, but the frontend renders nothing.

Medium effort

Planet Orbit View discoverability — The immersive planet view (/planet/:id) is only reachable by double-clicking a planet on the System View canvas. Add "View Planet" buttons on planet scan results and colony cards. The only way to navigate back is the Escape key, which is undiscoverable — add a visible back button.

Low effort
03
Trading UX
P1 HIGH

The trading table works but lacks decision-support information. Players have no way to know where to sell at a profit without manually visiting ports and mentally comparing prices.

Trading is the primary money-making loop for new players. The game has 32 commodities across 6 categories, 8 port types with supply/demand dynamics, and a dynamic pricing system — but the UI presents this as a flat table of numbers. The player has to do all the economic reasoning in their head. The Market Data page has price history charts and cross-port comparisons, but there's no connection between "I see prices here" and "I should buy/sell there." Also, commodity volume (how much cargo space each unit takes) is defined in the backend config but never shown to the player — so a player might buy 100 units of Livestock (volume: 5) thinking it'll fit in 100 cargo, when it actually needs 500.

Profit/loss indicators — Per commodity: "Bought avg: 50 cr, sell here: 72 cr" with green/red coloring.

Medium effort

"Best Trades" panel — Show top 3 profitable buy→sell opportunities from the current port based on price data from previously visited ports.

Medium effort

Separate "Buy Max" / "Sell All" buttons — The current single Max button uses a heuristic: if you own the commodity it sets max sell; if you don't, it sets max buy. Confusing when you own some but want to buy more.

Low effort

Show commodity volume — Large volume items fill cargo faster. Livestock takes 5x the space of Electronics. This is invisible to the player — it's defined in the backend config but never displayed.

Low effort

Route planner — On the Market Data page: "Buy X at Port A, sell at Port B, estimated profit: Z credits" based on explored ports.

High effort
04
Combat Feedback & Engagement
P1 HIGH

Auto-resolve combat shows round-by-round text with fixed 1-second delays — the player just waits. Real-time socket combat exists but offers limited player agency. Post-combat feedback shows only credits looted.

Combat is one of the three core pillars (trade, fight, explore). The backend combat system is surprisingly deep: critical hits, shield penetration, component degradation, flee mechanics based on speed differential, and an XP system tied to damage dealt. But almost none of this richness surfaces in the UI. After a fight, the player sees "Loot: 150 Credits" and that's it — no XP earned, no component damage report, no notification that their Plasma Cannon degraded 2%. The game also has a real-time combat mode via WebSocket with a CombatHUD component, which is a solid foundation that could be enhanced.

Speed control for auto-resolve — Add 1x, 2x, 4x, and "Skip to Result" options so players can watch details or skip ahead.

Low effort

Visual damage indicators — Animate hull/shield bars down with screen shake or flash on critical hits.

Medium effort

Detailed loot breakdown — After combat: credits earned, XP gained, items dropped, component damage taken. Currently only credits are shown.

Low effort

Pre-engagement threat assessment — "Enemy estimated power: HIGH. Win probability: ~65%. Recommended: upgrade shields." The combat service already has the math for this.

Medium effort
05
Ship Management & Customization
P1 HIGH

Ship management is fragmented across 4 separate pages (Ships, Shipyard, Customize, Engineering) with no cross-linking. The Ship Designer has a broken template feature. The Ship Customizer has no visual preview. Fleet creation uses hidden gestures.

The ship is the player's avatar in this game — it's the thing they identify with most. Currently they need to visit 4 different pages to understand their ship's full state. The Shipyard's "Design Templates" feature lets you save and delete templates but has no "Load" or "Apply" button — so saved templates are permanently useless display items. The Ship Customizer lets you pick hull colors, skins, decals, and engine trails, but the "Preview" is just a list of hex codes — no visual rendering at all. Fleet creation requires Shift+drag on the galaxy map canvas, documented only in 10px gray text at the bottom of the Ship panel.

Quick-action buttons on Ship Status — "Repair Hull" → Engineering, "Upgrade Components" → Shipyard, "Customize" → Customizer.

Low effort

Component condition warnings — Show "3 components need repair" on the Ship Status page. Currently requires navigating to Engineering to discover degradation.

Low effort

Fix Ship Design Templates — Add a "Load/Apply Template" button. Currently templates can only be saved and deleted — the list is read-only with no way to apply a design.

Low effort

Ship Customizer visual preview — Replace the hex-code text summary with a rendered ship silhouette or 3D preview that updates as the player changes colors and options.

High effort

Fleet discoverability — Add an explicit "Create Fleet" button on the Ship panel. Show fleet controls prominently when selected on the map. Currently hidden behind Shift+drag gesture.

Low effort

Ship comparison in Shipyard — When browsing components, show before/after stat comparison (damage +5, accuracy -3).

Medium effort

"Buy New Ship" storefront — No visible UI exists for purchasing additional ships.

High effort
06
Colony Management
P2 MEDIUM

Colonies have deep systems (15 building types with production chains, 6 wonders, raids, power grids, workforce) but the UI presents them as a flat list of cards. Players can't see production bottlenecks, power balance, or workforce allocation.

The colony system is probably the deepest feature in the game. Buildings have tiered upgrades (Surface Mine → Deep Core Drill → Quantum Extractor), input/output production chains (Refinery: 10 Ore → 8 Refined Metals), power generation and consumption, workforce requirements, planet-type bonuses, and maintenance costs. On top of that, there are 6 Wonders with multi-phase construction. But the UI doesn't expose most of this complexity — there's no way to see if your colony is power-starved or labor-short without doing the math yourself from individual building stats.

Production overview — Per colony show net resource flow: "Producing: 20 Ore, 25 Water. Consuming: 10 Ore, 5 Water. Net: +10 Ore, +20 Water."

Medium effort

Power & workforce indicators — Summary bars showing "Power: 250/300 MW" and "Workforce: 400/500 used." Buildings consume both but there's no colony-level dashboard.

Low effort

Building tech-tree visualization — Show which buildings unlock which, with prerequisites, instead of trial-and-error discovery.

Medium effort

"Collect All" button — For multi-colony players. Currently requires individual collection per colony.

Low effort

Wonder construction UX — 6 wonder types with multi-phase construction need: phase progress visualization, cost for next phase, cumulative bonus being built toward.

Medium effort

Raid alerts — Prominent notification when a colony is under raid, with quick-link to respond.

Low effort
07
Progression Visibility
P0 CRITICAL

Level, XP, and skill points are only visible on the dedicated Progression page. There is zero ambient sense of growth, no notifications for level-ups, and no achievement system.

The game has a 20-level progression system with 8 skills, a tech tree with 7 technologies, and ship/component unlocks tied to levels. But XP is earned silently — after combat, trading, or exploration, the player gets no feedback that they gained XP. They level up silently. They earn skill points silently. This kills the dopamine loop that keeps players engaged. Every successful game makes progression feel constant: "+50 XP" floating text, level-up fanfares, "NEW UNLOCK" popups. Space Wars has all the systems but none of the feedback.

XP bar in sidebar/top bar — Always visible, showing progress toward next level. This single change makes every action feel like it matters.

Low effort

Toast notifications — For level ups, skill points earned, tech research completed, mission rewards, artifact discovery. Currently all these events are completely silent.

Low effort

Level-up "What's New" popup — "You've unlocked: Corvette ship class. You earned: 1 skill point." Makes leveling feel rewarding.

Low effort

Achievement system — "First Kill," "Traded 10,000 credits," "Colonized a planet," "Explored 50 sectors." Trophy case on Dashboard.

High effort
08
Social & Multiplayer
P2 MEDIUM

Corporations, Messaging, Chat, Factions, and PvP all exist but feel disconnected. No player list, no way to see who's online, chat is a hidden floating button, and messaging requires typing exact usernames.

For an MMO, social presence is what keeps players logging in. The game has real-time WebSocket chat, a full inbox/outbox messaging system, corporations with treasury and agreements, factions with wars, and PvP combat. But the "M" in MMO is invisible: you can't see other players in your sector, you can't tell if anyone is online, and the chat button blends into the background. The messaging compose modal requires typing the exact username with no autocomplete — if you misspell "xX_StarLord_Xx" you get an error. The corporation agreement proposal requires typing a raw Corporation ID number.

"Players in Sector" panel — On System View, show other players' ships, their faction, PvP status. Creates presence and interaction opportunities.

Medium effort

Visible chat — Move from hidden bottom-right button to a sidebar tab or persistent bottom bar.

Low effort

Messaging: autocomplete + reply — Add player name autocomplete in compose. Add "Reply" button on received messages (currently must manually compose and retype the sender's name).

Low effort

Corporation agreement picker — Replace raw Corporation ID input with a searchable picker from the leaderboard.

Low effort

Dashboard leaderboards — Top traders, combatants, richest players, biggest corporations.

Medium effort
09
Crafting & Automation UX
P0 CRITICAL

Crafting and Automation pages require players to know internal game IDs and provide minimal feedback. Trade route creation needs raw Sector IDs typed manually. Crafting doesn't show if you have the ingredients.

This is the single worst UX pattern in the entire application: raw numeric ID inputs. At least 5 different pages require the player to type a raw Sector ID, Corporation ID, or Port ID into a text field with no autocomplete, no search, and no map integration. The Automation trade route builder asks you to type "Sector 847" as a waypoint — how would a player even know that number? The mining run form asks for "Return Port Sector ID." The Outpost builder asks for a Sector ID. This pattern exists because the forms were built to match the API contract rather than the player's mental model. Players think in terms of "Alpha Centauri Trading Hub," not "sector_id: 847."

Crafting: ingredient availability — Show green/red indicators per ingredient based on the active ship's cargo. Currently lists ingredients but doesn't say if the player can actually craft.

Low effort

Human-readable crafting time — Display "5 minutes" instead of raw seconds. Show ETA on in-progress jobs.

Low effort

Replace all raw ID inputs with pickers — Automation waypoints: searchable dropdown of explored sectors or click-on-map. Mining return port: dropdown of sectors with ports. Outpost building: sector picker or right-click-on-map "Build Outpost Here."

Medium effort
10
NPC Interaction & Dialogue
P2 MEDIUM

The game has an AI-powered NPC dialogue system with voice support, personality traits, and scripted conversations — a major differentiating feature. But entry points are not obvious and errors fail silently.

This is one of the most unique features in the game. The backend has a full dialogue system with AI integration, personality traits (greedy, honorable, cunning, etc.), speech styles (pirate slang, military, cryptic), voice profiles, and even a voice input endpoint. NPCs can hail the player via WebSocket. But in the UI, hail notifications are easy to miss, there's no way to review past conversations, and if the AI service is down the dialogue just silently fails. NPC personality traits that the backend carefully generates are never shown to the player.

Prominent hail notifications — Add sound cue and pulsing indicator. Currently a small overlay easily missed.

Low effort

NPC action indicators on System View — Color-code NPCs by hostility (friendly/hostile/neutral) and show available actions (Hail, Attack, Trade) without requiring a click.

Low effort

Dialogue history — Players can't review past NPC conversations. Trade tips and quest hints are lost when the chat closes.

Medium effort

Show NPC personality — Display traits like "greedy," "honorable" in the hail notification or chat header.

Low effort

Graceful AI failure handling — If the dialogue AI service is unavailable, show a clear message instead of silently failing.

Low effort
11
Community Events & Factions
P2 MEDIUM

Events page takes raw contribution amounts with no guidance. Faction page shows standings and wars but offers no actionable gameplay.

Community events are a proven engagement mechanic (see: Destiny 2 community challenges, Elite Dangerous CGs). Your implementation has the foundation — progress bars, leaderboards, contribution tracking — but lacks the "pull" that makes players want to participate. The contribution input is just a blank number field with no context about what a reasonable amount is or what exactly is being contributed. The Faction page shows your standings and active wars, but there's nothing you can do about it from that page. It's read-only information with no "call to action."

Event contribution guidance — Show player's credits, suggest amounts (10%, 25%, 50%), clarify what's being contributed.

Low effort

Personal contribution rank on event card — Show your own rank and amount on the main card, not hidden behind the leaderboard modal.

Low effort

Faction actionable gameplay — Link to faction-specific missions, show how recent actions changed standing, suggest actions to improve reputation.

High effort

War contribution — Show what the player can do to help their faction's war effort.

Medium effort
12
Quality of Life
P1 HIGH

Dozens of small friction points that collectively degrade the experience: inconsistent error handling, no keyboard shortcuts, no sound, no connection indicator, broken mobile, and more.

Death by a thousand cuts. Each issue is minor, but together they make the game feel unfinished. The most egregious: error handling is different on every page. Some pages use auto-dismissing toasts (5 seconds), some use persistent error banners, and the PvP toggle in ShipPanel uses a raw browser alert() dialog. The WebSocket connection (critical for real-time combat, chat, and NPC hails) has no visible status indicator — if it disconnects, the player has no idea why combat stopped working. The sidebar is a fixed 264px with no collapse, making the game unusable on any screen under ~1200px wide.

Consistent error/notification system — Standardize on a single toast/notification component with severity levels. Replace all alert() calls.

Medium effort

Socket connection indicator — Small icon in sidebar showing real-time connection status. Critical for combat, chat, NPC hails.

Low effort

Auto-refuel option — "Refuel to max when docking" toggle.

Low effort

Keyboard shortcuts — M=map, T=trading, S=ship, Space=confirm.

Low effort

Sound effects — Zero audio feedback currently. Even basic UI sounds dramatically improve immersion.

Medium effort

Responsive/mobile layout — Collapsible sidebar with hamburger menu.

High effort

Session persistence — Save last page in localStorage so refresh returns to the same place.

Low effort

Bulk actions — Sell all cargo, dismiss unassigned crew, collect all colony resources.

Medium effort

Basic accessibility — ARIA labels, keyboard focus management, skip-navigation links.

Medium effort
13
Visual Polish & Immersion
P3 POLISH

The dark space theme is solid but static. Every player sees the same cyan accent regardless of faction. All ships use the same Rocket icon. No page transitions, no ambient life.

The visual design is already above average for a project at this stage — the holographic panel styles, neon accents, and Orbitron headings create a cohesive space aesthetic. These suggestions are about going from "good" to "immersive." The biggest wins here are low-effort: faction theming just means swapping CSS variables, and ship icons are just SVG swaps. The in-game wiki is surprisingly well-built with markdown articles, search, and nice styling — but no other page in the game ever links to it.

Faction theming — Tint accent colors per faction: blue for Terran, red for Zythian, purple for Automaton. Just swap CSS variables based on user.faction.

Low effort

Ship silhouette icons — Replace generic Rocket icon with per-ship-type SVGs. Even simple outlines add character.

Low effort

Dashboard ambient animations — Subtle star parallax or animated ship graphic.

Medium effort

Event/notification ticker — Scrolling feed: "Pirate activity in Sector 47," "Trade prices shifted at Mining Outpost 12."

Medium effort

Wiki cross-linking — Add "Learn more" links and tooltips from game pages to wiki articles. The wiki is well-built but invisible.

Low effort

Page transition animations — Quick warp/fade effect between major sections.

Low effort
