Our Legacy

Standing on the shoulders of giants.

Inspired by TradeWars 2002

Space Wars 3000 draws inspiration from the legendary TradeWars 2002, one of the first multiplayer space trading games that captivated players in the BBS era of the 1980s and 1990s. Before the internet was widely available to the public, players would dial into Bulletin Board Systems to explore the universe, trade commodities, and engage in space combat.

TradeWars 2002 created a sense of community as players plotted sector maps on paper, formed alliances, and developed strategies to dominate the galaxy. Some dedicated players would even hang physical maps on their walls, meticulously tracking the locations of ports, planets, and enemy territories.

"TradeWars 2002 wasn't just a game - it was a social experience that brought together players from across the world in a shared universe of endless possibilities."

Space Wars 3000 honors this legacy while bringing the concept into the modern era with enhanced graphics, expanded gameplay mechanics, and a persistent online universe. We've preserved the core elements that made TradeWars 2002 special while adding new features that today's players expect.

Whether you're a nostalgic veteran of the original game or a newcomer to space trading games, Space Wars 3000 offers an immersive experience that captures the spirit of exploration, trade, and conquest that defined its legendary predecessor.

The Legacy of TradeWars

1980s
The Birth of a Legend

TradeWars 2002 emerged in the late 1980s as one of the first multiplayer space trading games, running on Bulletin Board Systems (BBS) before the internet was widely available to the public. Players would dial in with modems to take their turns in this text-based universe.

1990s
Golden Age of BBS Gaming

Throughout the 1990s, TradeWars 2002 became the most popular BBS game of its time. Players would meticulously plot sector maps on paper, form alliances via phone calls, and develop complex strategies to dominate the digital universe.

2000s
The Internet Revolution

As the internet replaced BBS systems, TradeWars 2002 maintained a dedicated following. Various attempts were made to bring the game to modern platforms, but none captured the magic of the original experience.

Present
A New Beginning

Space Wars 3000 emerges as the spiritual successor to TradeWars 2002, combining the strategic depth of the original with modern graphics, real-time gameplay, and expanded features for a new generation of space adventurers.
