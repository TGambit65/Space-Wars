Command multiple ships in large-scale space battles. Master fleet formations, coordinate attacks, and manage complex multi-ship operations for decisive tactical victories.

Battle Formations

Formation Doctrine: Ship positioning affects firepower, defense, and maneuverability.
⭐ Line Formations

Battle Line Formation

Structure: Ships arranged in single or double line

  • Advantages: Maximum forward firepower, coordinated volleys
  • Disadvantages: Vulnerable to flanking attacks
  • Best Use: Long-range engagements, capital ship duels
  • Ship Types: Battleships and heavy cruisers

Echelon Formation

Structure: Diagonal line, ships offset from each other

  • Advantages: Good firepower, can pivot to face threats
  • Disadvantages: Complex coordination required
  • Best Use: Advancing on fortified positions
  • Ship Types: Mixed capital and escort ships
FIREPOWER Line formations maximize broadside capability
WEAKNESS Flanks are vulnerable to fast attack
🛡️ Defensive Formations

Defensive Sphere

Structure: Ships arranged in 3D sphere around central objective

  • Advantages: 360-degree coverage, mutual support
  • Disadvantages: Reduced forward firepower
  • Best Use: Protecting stations, convoys, or capitals
  • Ship Types: Mix of interceptors and point defense ships

Layered Defense

Structure: Multiple defensive rings at different ranges

  • Outer Ring: Scouts and long-range interceptors
  • Middle Ring: Main combat ships
  • Inner Ring: Point defense and close escort
  • Core: Protected asset or command ship
⚔️ Assault Formations

Spearhead Formation

Structure: Wedge-shaped formation with heavy ships at point

  • Point Ships: Heavily armored breakthrough vessels
  • Support Ships: Follow-up forces for exploitation
  • Advantages: Concentrated breakthrough power
  • Disadvantages: Vulnerable to coordinated counterattack

Pincer Movement

Structure: Two or more groups attack from different angles

  • Primary Force: Main frontal assault
  • Flanking Forces: Attack enemy sides or rear
  • Timing: Coordinated simultaneous attack
  • Goal: Overwhelm enemy ability to respond

Ship Roles and Specialization

🚢 Capital Ships

Heavy Combat
  • Battleships: Main battle line, heavy firepower
  • Dreadnoughts: Super-heavy assault ships
  • Carriers: Fighter/bomber launch platforms
  • Command Ships: Fleet coordination and control

Role: Anchor defensive lines, breakthrough enemy formations

⚡ Escort Ships

Support
  • Destroyers: Anti-fighter, anti-missile defense
  • Frigates: Patrol, escort, screening duties
  • Corvettes: Fast attack, reconnaissance
  • Cruisers: Independent operations, fire support

Role: Protect capitals, screen formations, exploit weaknesses

🚀 Strike Craft

Fast Attack
  • Fighters: Air superiority, escort missions
  • Bombers: Anti-capital ship attacks
  • Interceptors: High-speed interception
  • Assault Craft: Boarding and close combat

Role: Swarm tactics, precision strikes, harassment

🔧 Support Vessels

Logistics
  • Repair Ships: Field maintenance and recovery
  • Supply Ships: Ammunition and fuel resupply
  • Hospital Ships: Medical treatment and evacuation
  • Electronic Warfare: Jamming and intelligence

Role: Sustain fleet operations, force multipliers

Large-Scale Tactical Operations

🎯 Target Prioritization

Primary Target Categories

  1. Command Ships: Disrupt enemy coordination
  2. Carriers: Eliminate strike craft threat
  3. Heavy Damage Dealers: Reduce enemy firepower
  4. Support Vessels: Degrade enemy sustainability
  5. Weakened Ships: Eliminate damaged targets
FOCUS FIRE Concentrate multiple ships on single target
PRIORITY Eliminate threats in order of danger
EFFICIENCY Destroy rather than merely damage
⚔️ Combined Arms Operations

Multi-Role Coordination

  • Capital Ship Bombardment: Soften enemy defenses
  • Fighter Sweeps: Clear enemy strike craft
  • Bomber Strikes: Precision attacks on priority targets
  • Escort Screen: Protect bombers during attack runs

Timing and Synchronization

  • Phase 1: Establish space superiority
  • Phase 2: Suppress enemy defenses
  • Phase 3: Execute primary assault
  • Phase 4: Exploitation and pursuit
🌊 Fleet Maneuvers

Classic Naval Maneuvers Adapted for Space

  • Crossing the T: Position fleet perpendicular to enemy line
  • Envelopment: Surround enemy fleet with superior numbers
  • Breakthrough: Penetrate enemy line at weak point
  • Feint and Flanking: Distract while main force attacks elsewhere

3D Space Considerations

  • Vertical Advantage: Attack from above or below enemy plane
  • Multi-Axis Attack: Coordinate attacks from multiple directions
  • Spatial Positioning: Use 3D space to avoid return fire
  • Escape Routes: Maintain multiple retreat vectors

Example Fleet Engagements

⚔️ Scenario: Convoy Escort vs. Raider Fleet
Tactical Situation Analysis
ESCORT 1 Heavy Cruiser, 4 Destroyers, 12 Freighters
RAIDERS 3 Light Cruisers, 8 Corvettes
OBJECTIVE Escort: Protect convoy | Raiders: Capture cargo

Escort Strategy

  • Form defensive sphere around convoy
  • Heavy cruiser provides long-range fire support
  • Destroyers screen against corvette attacks
  • Freighters maintain course while escort fights

Raider Strategy

  • Corvettes swarm and separate escort ships
  • Cruisers target heavy escort ships
  • Fast hit-and-run tactics to avoid prolonged engagement
  • Attempt to disable rather than destroy valuable cargo
🏰 Scenario: Capital Ship Duel
Fleet Engagement Parameters
FLEET A 2 Battleships, 1 Carrier, 6 Escorts
FLEET B 1 Dreadnought, 2 Cruisers, 8 Escorts
TERRAIN Asteroid field with multiple hiding spots

Fleet A Advantages

  • Carrier provides fighter/bomber support
  • Two battleships can focus fire
  • Numerical advantage in total firepower

Fleet B Advantages

  • Dreadnought has superior individual firepower
  • More maneuverable force composition
  • Can use asteroids for cover and ambush

Fleet Command Best Practices

Strategic Principles

  • Unity of Command: Clear chain of command
  • Concentration of Force: Focus superior numbers at decisive point
  • Economy of Force: Use minimum force for secondary objectives
  • Flexibility: Adapt to changing tactical situation
  • Communication: Maintain constant coordination

Common Mistakes

  • Micromanagement: Over-controlling individual ships
  • Formation Rigidity: Failing to adapt to situation
  • Target Dispersal: Spreading fire across too many targets
  • Poor Communication: Losing coordination during battle
  • Ignoring Support: Not protecting logistics and support ships
Fleet Command: Success requires balancing strategic vision with tactical flexibility.
