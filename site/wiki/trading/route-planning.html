Route Planning Guide

Navigation Computer: Efficient route planning maximizes profit per time unit.

Master the art of planning efficient trading routes. Learn to balance distance, fuel costs, cargo capacity, and risk to maximize your trading profits.

Route Planning Fundamentals

Route Optimization System
OBJECTIVE Maximize: (Revenue - Costs) / Time
VARIABLES Distance, Fuel, Cargo Value, Risk, Market Timing
CONSTRAINTS Ship capacity, Fuel range, Market availability
🗺️ Basic Route Types

Point-to-Point Routes

Best for: High-value, single commodity trading

  • Direct path between two sectors
  • Minimize travel time and fuel cost
  • Simple profit calculation
  • Lower risk exposure

Circuit Routes

Best for: Multi-commodity trading with regular schedules

  • Visit multiple sectors in sequence
  • Buy and sell different commodities at each stop
  • Return to starting point to repeat
  • Maximize cargo utilization

Opportunistic Routes

Best for: Experienced traders with market knowledge

  • Flexible routing based on current market conditions
  • Adapt to emerging opportunities
  • Higher potential profits but more complex
  • Requires excellent market intelligence
⚡ Fuel Efficiency Planning

Fuel is both a cost and a constraint. Plan fuel consumption carefully:

FORMULA Fuel_Cost = Distance × Ship_Consumption × Fuel_Price
RANGE Max_Distance = Fuel_Capacity / Consumption_Rate
SAFETY Always plan 20% fuel reserve for emergencies

Fuel Optimization Strategies

  • Fuel Stops: Plan refueling at cheapest fuel stations
  • Ship Configuration: Use fuel-efficient engines for long routes
  • Cargo Weight: Heavy cargo increases fuel consumption
  • Flight Paths: Direct routes save fuel vs. detours

Route Planning Tools and Techniques

📡 Navigation Tools

  • Sector Maps: Physical layout of space regions
  • Navigation Computer: Automated route calculation
  • Trade Scanner: Real-time commodity pricing
  • Communication Array: Market intelligence network

📊 Analysis Methods

  • Profit Per Jump: Revenue divided by route segments
  • Time Efficiency: Credits earned per hour
  • Risk Assessment: Probability-weighted returns
  • Opportunity Cost: Compare alternative routes

Manual Route Planning Process

Planning Procedure
STEP 1 Identify profitable price differences between sectors
STEP 2 Calculate distance and fuel requirements
STEP 3 Assess cargo capacity vs. commodity availability
STEP 4 Evaluate security risks along route
STEP 5 Calculate net profit and time requirements
STEP 6 Compare with alternative routes

Risk Assessment and Mitigation

Risk Factors: Higher profit routes often involve higher risks.
🚨 Security Risks

Pirate Activity Zones

  • Border Regions: Areas between major faction territories
  • Asteroid Fields: Natural hiding places for ambush
  • Remote Sectors: Low patrol frequency areas
  • Trade Lanes: High-traffic routes attract pirates

Risk Mitigation Strategies

  • Convoy Travel: Travel with other traders for protection
  • Escort Hire: Pay for military protection
  • Route Variation: Use unpredictable paths
  • Speed Builds: Fast ships can outrun threats
  • Low Profile: Avoid carrying obviously valuable cargo
⚠️ Market Risks

Price Volatility Factors

  • Long Transit Times: Markets can change during travel
  • Competition: Other traders affecting prices
  • Market Events: Sudden supply/demand changes
  • Information Lag: Outdated market intelligence
RULE Longer routes = Higher price volatility risk
STRATEGY Diversify cargo to spread market risk
🔧 Operational Risks

Equipment and Navigation Hazards

  • Mechanical Failures: Engine or system breakdowns
  • Navigation Errors: Getting lost or taking wrong routes
  • Environmental Hazards: Stellar phenomena, asteroid fields
  • Fuel Shortages: Running out of fuel mid-route

Operational Risk Prevention

  • Maintenance: Keep ship in good repair
  • Backup Systems: Redundant navigation and communication
  • Emergency Supplies: Extra fuel, parts, and provisions
  • Route Documentation: Detailed flight plans and backups

Advanced Route Strategies

🔄 Multi-Stop Optimization

Cargo Chain Planning

  • Buy at production center
  • Sell at consumption center
  • Buy different commodity for return trip
  • Maximize cargo space utilization

Example: Agricultural world → Urban center → Industrial world → Agricultural world

⏰ Timing Optimization

Market Timing Strategies

  • Arrive during high-demand periods
  • Avoid oversupplied markets
  • Coordinate with production cycles
  • Account for travel time in planning
Tip: Factor in 1-2 hour buffer for market changes.

Route Optimization Algorithms

SIMPLE Highest profit margin per jump
TIME Highest profit per hour traveled
RISK Risk-adjusted returns (profit × safety)
CAPACITY Highest profit per cargo unit
