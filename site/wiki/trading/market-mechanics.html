Market Mechanics

Market Analysis: Understanding supply and demand patterns is essential for profitable trading.

Master the underlying economic systems that drive commodity prices across the galaxy. Learn how supply, demand, and market events create trading opportunities.

Supply and Demand Fundamentals

Economic Analysis System
PRINCIPLE Price = Base_Value × (Demand/Supply) × Market_Factors
SUPPLY Local production + Imports - Recent sales
DEMAND Population needs + Industrial consumption
📈 Price Calculation Mechanics

Base Price Modifiers

  • Abundance (150%+ supply): 50-70% of base price
  • Normal (80-150% supply): 80-120% of base price
  • Shortage (50-80% supply): 120-180% of base price
  • Critical (0-50% supply): 180-300% of base price
EXAMPLE Food (base 10cr) in shortage = 10 × 1.5 = 15cr
EXAMPLE Art (base 100cr) in abundance = 100 × 0.6 = 60cr
⚖️ Market Balance Dynamics

Markets naturally tend toward equilibrium, but external factors constantly disrupt this balance:

Equilibrium Factors

  • Production Rates: How quickly sectors generate commodities
  • Consumption Rates: How quickly populations use commodities
  • Trade Volume: Player and NPC trading activity
  • Transport Efficiency: How easily goods move between sectors
Market Memory: Recent trading activity affects prices for 24-48 hours.

Production and Consumption Centers

Different sector types specialize in producing or consuming specific commodities:

🏭 Production Centers

Low Prices
  • Agricultural Worlds: Food, organic materials
  • Mining Colonies: Raw metals, minerals
  • Industrial Sectors: Manufactured goods, machinery
  • Tech Centers: Electronics, advanced components
  • Energy Stations: Fuel, power cells

🏢 Consumption Centers

High Prices
  • Urban Centers: Food, luxury goods, entertainment
  • Military Bases: Weapons, armor, medical supplies
  • Research Stations: Scientific equipment, rare materials
  • Tourist Destinations: Art, entertainment, exotic foods
  • Construction Sites: Raw materials, machinery

Identifying Market Opportunities

PROFIT Find production centers with surplus goods
DEMAND Locate consumption centers with shortages
ROUTE Plan efficient path between source and destination
TIMING Execute trades before market conditions change

Market Events and Fluctuations

Dynamic Markets: Events can rapidly change supply and demand patterns.

Event Types and Market Impact

🚨 Crisis Events

High Impact
  • Pirate Attacks: Disrupt supply chains
  • Natural Disasters: Destroy production facilities
  • Equipment Failures: Halt production temporarily
  • Population Growth: Increase demand suddenly

📈 Growth Events

Moderate Impact
  • New Colonies: Create fresh markets
  • Tech Breakthroughs: Change production efficiency
  • Trade Agreements: Alter market relationships
  • Infrastructure: Improve transport efficiency

Seasonal and Cyclical Patterns

  • Agricultural Cycles: Food prices vary with harvest times
  • Industrial Schedules: Predictable production and maintenance cycles
  • Holiday Periods: Increased demand for luxury goods
  • Military Exercises: Temporary spikes in weapons and supplies

Interactive Trading Mechanics

💰 Transaction Mechanics

Buy/Sell Process

  1. Price Discovery: Check current market prices at port
  2. Volume Assessment: Determine available quantity
  3. Transaction Execution: Complete purchase/sale
  4. Market Impact: Your trade affects future prices
IMPACT Large purchases increase prices
IMPACT Large sales decrease prices
RECOVERY Markets gradually return to equilibrium
📊 Market Information Systems

Available Market Data

  • Current Prices: Real-time pricing at your location
  • Price History: Recent price trends and patterns
  • Volume Data: Available quantity for purchase/sale
  • Market Rumors: Hints about upcoming events
Information Advantage: Advanced sensors and communication systems provide better market intelligence.
⏰ Timing Strategies

Optimal Trading Windows

  • Buy Low Periods: Post-harvest, after large shipments arrive
  • Sell High Periods: Pre-shortage, during crisis events
  • Avoid Rush Periods: When many traders compete simultaneously
  • Monitor Cycles: Learn predictable market rhythms

Advanced Market Concepts

Market Manipulation and Ethics

Market Influence: Large traders can significantly impact smaller markets.
  • Monopolization: Controlling supply of critical commodities
  • Price Fixing: Coordinating with other traders (risky)
  • Market Cornering: Buying all available stock to drive prices up
  • Speculation: Trading on rumors and future events

Market Intelligence

  • Economic Espionage: Gathering information about competitor strategies
  • Trend Analysis: Identifying patterns in price movements
  • Event Prediction: Anticipating market-moving events
  • Network Building: Establishing information sources
