Profit Calculation Guide

Financial Computer: Accurate profit calculations are essential for successful trading operations.

Master the mathematics of trading profits. Learn to calculate margins, factor in all costs, and optimize your return on investment across all trading activities.

Basic Profit Calculation

Profit Analysis System
GROSS Gross_Profit = (Sell_Price - Buy_Price) × Quantity
NET Net_Profit = Gross_Profit - Total_Costs
MARGIN Profit_Margin = (Net_Profit / Revenue) × 100%
ROI ROI = (Net_Profit / Investment) × 100%
💰 Simple Trade Example

Basic Food Trade Calculation

BUY 100 units Food @ 8 credits = 800 credits
SELL 100 units Food @ 12 credits = 1,200 credits
FUEL 4 jumps × 10 fuel × 2 credits = 80 credits
FEES Port fees: 20 credits
NET PROFIT 1,200 - 800 - 80 - 20 = 300 credits
  • Revenue: 1,200 credits
  • Investment: 900 credits (purchase + costs)
  • Profit Margin: 25% (300/1,200)
  • ROI: 33.3% (300/900)

Cost Analysis and Tracking

Hidden Costs: Failing to account for all costs is the most common profit calculation error.
⚡ Direct Trading Costs

Fuel Costs

  • Travel Fuel: Distance × Ship_Consumption × Fuel_Price
  • Cargo Impact: Heavy loads increase fuel consumption
  • Efficiency Rating: Ship engines affect fuel efficiency
  • Emergency Reserve: Always budget extra fuel for safety

Port and Transaction Fees

  • Docking Fees: 10-50 credits per port visit
  • Transaction Fees: 1-3% of transaction value
  • Storage Fees: If cargo sits in port warehouses
  • Inspection Fees: Security scans for certain cargo types
FORMULA Direct_Costs = Fuel + Port_Fees + Transaction_Fees
🔧 Operational Costs

Ship Maintenance

  • Component Wear: Regular maintenance costs
  • Hull Integrity: Structural repairs from normal use
  • System Updates: Software and navigation updates
  • Emergency Repairs: Unexpected breakdowns

Insurance and Security

  • Cargo Insurance: Protection against loss
  • Ship Insurance: Hull and equipment coverage
  • Security Escorts: Hired protection for dangerous routes
  • Bribes: Unofficial "fees" in certain sectors
Tip: Amortize maintenance costs across multiple trading runs.
⏰ Time and Opportunity Costs

Time Value Analysis

  • Hourly Rate: Your desired credits per hour
  • Travel Time: Time spent moving between sectors
  • Loading Time: Cargo handling and paperwork
  • Waiting Time: Market timing and delays
EFFICIENCY Profit_Per_Hour = Net_Profit / Total_Time_Hours
TARGET Aim for 1,000+ credits per hour for sustainable trading

Advanced Profit Optimization

📈 Margin Optimization

High-Margin Strategies

  • Luxury Goods: Higher margins but lower volume
  • Specialty Items: Unique or rare commodities
  • Time-Sensitive: Emergency or urgent deliveries
  • Risk Premium: Dangerous routes with hazard pay

🔄 Volume Optimization

High-Volume Strategies

  • Bulk Commodities: Lower margins but high volume
  • Regular Routes: Predictable, repeatable trades
  • Cargo Maximization: Fill every available space
  • Quick Turnover: Fast loading/unloading cycles

Portfolio Approach to Trading

DIVERSIFY Mix high-margin and high-volume trades
BALANCE Risk/reward ratio across multiple commodities
HEDGE Counter-cyclical commodities to reduce volatility

Risk-Adjusted Profit Analysis

Risk Premium: Higher-risk trades should provide proportionally higher returns.
⚖️ Risk Assessment Framework

Risk Factors and Multipliers

  • Pirate Risk (1.2-2.0x): Danger zones require risk premium
  • Market Volatility (1.1-1.5x): Unstable prices increase uncertainty
  • Cargo Value (1.0-1.3x): Valuable cargo attracts thieves
  • Route Distance (1.0-1.2x): Longer routes have more variables
FORMULA Risk_Adjusted_Return = Expected_Profit × (1 - Risk_Factor)
EXAMPLE 10,000cr profit × (1 - 0.15 risk) = 8,500cr expected
🎯 Expected Value Calculations

Probability-Weighted Returns

Calculate expected profit considering various outcomes:

SUCCESS 80% chance: +15,000 credits
PROBLEMS 15% chance: +5,000 credits
DISASTER 5% chance: -20,000 credits
EXPECTED (0.8×15,000) + (0.15×5,000) + (0.05×-20,000) = 11,750 credits

Profit Tracking and Performance Analysis

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

📊 Financial Metrics

  • Net Profit per Trade: Average profit per transaction
  • Profit Margin %: Efficiency of pricing strategy
  • ROI %: Return on invested capital
  • Credits per Hour: Time efficiency metric

⚡ Operational Metrics

  • Cargo Utilization %: How full your cargo holds are
  • Route Efficiency: Profit per jump or distance unit
  • Market Timing: Success rate of price predictions
  • Risk Incidents: Frequency of losses or problems
