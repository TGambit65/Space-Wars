📖 Comprehensive Glossary of Terms

Space Dictionary! All the terms, acronyms, and space slang you need to know to sound like a pro pilot. From A to Z! 🔤

Ever been confused when someone yells "Check your six!" or wondered what the heck a "jump drive capacitor flux inhibitor" is? This glossary has all the answers! We've collected every term, abbreviation, and piece of space slang used in Space Wars 3000. Now you can talk like a veteran pilot even on your first day! 🚀

🔍 Search Terms

A

Afterburner

Engine boost that makes you go "ZOOM!" but drinks fuel like a thirsty camel. Great for escaping pirates or showing off.

Equipment Common

AI (Artificial Intelligence)

The smart computer brains that run the Nexus Syndicate. They're surprisingly good at jokes and terrible at understanding sarcasm.

Faction Common

Armor

The tough outer shell that keeps your squishy bits from becoming space dust. Like a really expensive metal jacket.

Defense Common

B

Battleship

The biggest, baddest ships in space. Slow as molasses but hits like a freight train carrying other freight trains.

Ship Class Common

Bob

The friendliest pirate in the galaxy. Seriously, he's just trying to pay off his student loans from pirate school.

NPC Legendary

Bogey

Unidentified ship on radar. Could be friendly, could be hostile, could be Bob looking for directions.

Combat Term Military

C

Credits

Space money! The universal currency accepted everywhere except that one vending machine on Station 7 that only takes exact change.

Currency Universal

Critical Hit

When your shot is so good it does extra damage. Like hitting the exact spot where they keep their snacks.

Combat Gaming

D

DPS (Damage Per Second)

How much hurt you can dish out every second. Higher is better, unless you're on the receiving end!

Combat Stat Common

Docking

Parking your ship at a station. Harder than parallel parking, but at least there's no meter maids in space.

Navigation Common

F

Faction

The major groups in the galaxy. Like clubs, but with more lasers and political intrigue.

Game System Common

Fuel

The juice that makes your ship go. Without it, you're just an expensive floating decoration.

Resource Essential

J

Jump Drive

The magic device that lets you teleport between sectors. Makes a cool "whoosh" sound that's totally necessary for operation.

Equipment Essential

Juice

Pilot slang for fuel, energy, or whatever keeps things running. "We're out of juice!" sounds cooler than "we need gas!"

Slang Casual

N

Noob

New player. We all were one once! (Some of us still are, shh don't tell)

Player Term Gaming

NPC (Non-Player Character)

Computer-controlled characters like traders, pirates, and that guy who keeps asking if you've seen his space hamster.

Game Term Universal

P

Pew Pew

The official sound lasers make in space. Scientists confirm this is 100% accurate.

Sound Effect Universal

Pirate

Space outlaws who want your stuff. They have eye patches even though laser eye surgery is readily available.

Enemy Type Common

S

Shields

Energy barriers that protect your ship. Like a force field, but real and powered by space magic (and reactors).

Defense Essential

Six (Check Your Six)

Behind you! Based on clock positions. "Check your six" means look behind you, usually because something wants to blow you up.

Combat Call Military

🗣️ Space Slang Dictionary

"Burn and Turn"

Hit afterburners and make a sharp turn. Classic evasive maneuver or way to look cool.

"Space Dust"

What you become when your hull hits zero. Not recommended.

"Milk Run"

An easy mission or trade route. Named after ancient Earth delivery routes.

"Sitting Duck"

A ship that's not moving or defending itself. Easy target!

"Full Burn"

Maximum speed ahead! Usually followed by "out of fuel" messages.
