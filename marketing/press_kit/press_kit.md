## Description

Space Wars 3000 is a browser-based multiplayer space trading and combat
game built around a single, unified real-time combat engine. Players
explore a persistent galaxy of 1,200 sectors, trade commodities through a
live economy of 800+ ports, design custom ships, and fight other captains
in PvP duels, arenas, and bounty contracts.

What sets it apart: every enemy ship you destroy leaves a fully explorable
derelict wreck. You can board it on foot, walk its decks, and salvage
credits, cargo, and components — and so can anyone else who arrives in
time. PvE, PvP, and spectator combat all share one ruleset, so progression
in any mode counts everywhere.

---

## Key features

- **Real-time tactical combat** — A fixed-step 10 Hz engine drives PvE,
  PvP duels, arena matches, and bounty contracts under one ruleset.
- **Walkable derelict wrecks** — Every NPC kill produces a 2D
  ship-interior you can board, walk, and loot. First captain to the
  wreck wins the spoils.
- **Living economy** — 33 commodities flow through 800+ ports across
  1,200 sectors. Supply and demand actually move prices.
- **Ship designer** — Mix hulls, weapons, shields, scanners, and
  utility components to build a vessel that matches your playstyle.
- **Bounty board, arena lobby & spectator view** — Put a price on a
  rival's head, queue for ranked duels, or watch live battles unfold.
- **Reconnect-safe** — A dropped session won't end your fight. Your
  ship goes on autopilot until you're back.
- **Faction warfare & colonies** — Claim sectors, build colonies, and
  contest territory with rival factions.

## History (short version)

Space Wars 3000 grew out of a love for the classic browser-based space
trading games of the late 90s — TradeWars, Universal Combat, the early
EVE Online beta — combined with the modern desire for real-time tactical
combat and persistent multiplayer worlds. Built in the open as a Replit
project, the game shipped after a series of public combat shakedowns
that unified PvE and PvP under a single tick-driven engine, then layered
walkable boarding on top of every kill.
